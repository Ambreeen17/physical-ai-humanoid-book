## Joint Space vs Configuration Space

### ASCII Representation

```
                    Configuration Space (C-Space)
                    =============================

                         q2 (Joint 2 Angle)
                              ^
                              |     /|
                              |    / |
                              |   /  |
                              |  /   |   Reachable
                              | /    |   Configurations
                              |/     |
    -150 deg  ---------------+-------+----------> q1 (Joint 1 Angle)
                     \       |       /
                      \      |      /
                       \     |     /
                        \    |    /
                         \   |   /   Joint Limits
                          \  |  /    Applied
                           \ | /
                            \|/
                             v

                    Inner shaded region = C-Space
                    Outer boundary = Joint Space (unbounded)

    ============================================================

                    Physical Interpretation
                    =======================

    Joint Space (unconstrained):
    ---------------------------
          q1 axis: [-180, +180] degrees (full rotation)
          q2 axis: [-180, +180] degrees (full rotation)

    Configuration Space (with limits):
    ---------------------------------
          q1 axis: [-120, +120] degrees (limited by shoulder)
          q2 axis: [-150, +150] degrees (limited by elbow)

    C-Space Shape:
    - Unconstrained: Torus (n-dimensional)
    - Constrained: Hyper-rectangle with collision cutouts

    ============================================================

    Unitree G1 Arm (6-DOF):
    ----------------------
          q = [q1, q2, q3, q4, q5, q6]^T

          Joint Space: R^6 (6-dimensional real space)
          Configuration Space: Bounded subset of R^6

          Dimension: 6
          Shape: 6D hyper-rectangle with constraints
```

### Description for Docusaurus

**Alt Text:**
Diagram showing the relationship between joint space, configuration space, and workspace for robot manipulators. Joint space is the full n-dimensional space of joint angles; configuration space is the constrained subset with joint limits applied; workspace shows reachable end-effector positions.

**Caption:**
Joint space represents all possible joint angle combinations, while configuration space (C-space) constrains this with physical limits. The Unitree G1's 6-DOF arm has a 6-dimensional joint space but a bounded configuration space due to joint limits and collision avoidance.

**Explanation:**
The diagram illustrates three fundamental spaces in robotics. Joint space is the n-dimensional space where each axis represents one joint angle; for the Unitree G1 arm, this is R^6. Configuration space (C-space) constrains joint space with physical limitsâ€”for revolute joints with limits, C-space becomes a bounded region. The workspace describes reachable end-effector positions, which is the image of C-space under forward kinematics. A key insight is that the mapping from C-space to workspace is many-to-one: multiple configurations can reach the same end-effector position (e.g., elbow-up vs elbow-down poses).
